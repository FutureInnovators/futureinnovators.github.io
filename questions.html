Competition details including dates, rules, and guidelines will be finalized shortly, so check back often! The rules and guidelines below should serve as a springboard and overview. Be sure to submit your email address to be notified about the latest updates.
What is a pitch competition?
Pitch competitions are a place to present your business ideas. They provide an opportunity for entrepreneurs to receive feedback, funding, and networking to advance their business ventures. Several teams or individuals present their ideas to experienced judges and receive constructive feedback with the goal of identifying opportunities and launching new ventures.
How do I create a pitch presentation?
To help provide guidelines for participants, informational videos will be posted in the coming months. These videos will support participants in crafting their strongest entries by discussing a variety of topics such as idea generation, opportunity identification, and how to craft an excellent pitch.
Are there competition and submission guidelines?

Students may work alone or in teams of two to three members. All team members must be from the same region of Indiana (see this link for region details). The competition will consist of three rounds:

Round one will consist of an electronically submitted video pitch and typed executive summary. Submissions for round one must be submitted on or before February 10th.

Round two will be a live pitch in front of a panel of judges; participants must qualify for this round based on placement in round one. The top ten pitches per region will compete in a live regional competition.

Round three will be a live pitch in front of a panel of judges; participants must qualify for this round based on placement in round two. The winners from each of the nine regions will come together to pitch to a panel of experts in Indianapolis this April.

Students must submit an executive summary outlining their business venture along with their application.
