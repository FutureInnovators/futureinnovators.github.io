Future Innovators of Indiana Pitch Competition
Competition details including dates, rules, and guidelines will become finalized in the coming weeks as we continue to formulate this opportunity. The below rules and guidelines are not all-inclusive. Rather, this information serves as a springboard and overview. Be sure to join our mailing list to be notified of more details over the coming months.
What is a pitch competition?
Pitch competitions are a place to present your business plans. They provide an opportunity for entrepreneurs to access feedback, funding, and networking to advance their business ventures. Several teams or individuals present their ideas to experienced judges and receive constructive feedback with the goal of improving and actualizing these business platforms.
How do I create a pitch presentation?
As an aid for participants, informational videos will be posted in the coming months.  These videos will support participants in crafting their strongest entries by discussing a variety of topics such as entrepreneurship basics, idea generation, industry, marketing, competitor analyses, and pitch writing.
Are there competition and submission guidelines?
Students may work alone or in teams of two to three members. All team members must be from the same region of Indiana (see this link for region details). The competition will consist of two rounds: Round one will consist of an electronically submitted video pitch and written outline. Submissions for round one must be turned in no later than February 10th. Round two will be a live pitch in front of a panel of judges; participants must qualify for this round based on placement in round one. This will occur in Indianapolis this April. Students must submit an executive summary outlining their business venture along with their application.
